    About the procedure

    What happens during the colonoscopy

    You'll be given a sedative through a vein in your arm. It makes you comfortable and slightly drowsy — most people have little or no memory of the test.

    The colonoscope is a thin flexible tube (about the thickness of your index finger). It's inserted through the anus and gently guided along the large bowel. The procedure takes about 30 minutes.

    If any polyps are found, they're usually removed during the procedure (polypectomy).

    Safety & risks

    Serious complications are uncommon — about 1 in 1000. They include:

    • Intolerance of the bowel prep solution
    • Reaction to the sedative
    • Major bleeding or bowel perforation (more likely if polyps are removed)

    Occasionally these may require treatment by blood transfusion or surgery.

    After the procedure

    You'll be in recovery for 1–2 hours until the sedative wears off. When fully awake, you'll be told the results.

    Tell recovery staff immediately if you have severe abdominal pain, fever, bleeding, or any new symptoms. These can also develop a few days later — call Dr Majid immediately (0488 379 080) if they do.

    For 24 hours after: no driving, no signing legal documents, no operating dangerous machinery. A responsible adult must stay with you overnight.
